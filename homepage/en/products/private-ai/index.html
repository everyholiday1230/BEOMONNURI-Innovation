Beomon Private AI

An in-house AI that keeps sensitive data from leaving

Beomon Private AI is an in-house AI solution that lets you search and use enterprise knowledge assets — internal documents, policies, manuals, and reports — within a secure environment. By designing permissions, logs, and the data boundary together, it reduces security burden and improves practical usability.

Problem

The problem

The need to use generative AI is growing, but entering internal documents and customer data into external services carries heavy security and compliance burden. As a result, official adoption is delayed and uncontrolled, unofficial use can occur.

Solution

How we solve it

Beomon Private AI first designs a structure where data stays within your control. It combines document search, answer generation, access control, and usage-history management to provide an in-house AI environment staff can use with confidence.

Features

Key features

  • Search-based answers over internal documents, policies, and manuals
  • Answers grounded in source documents
  • Role-based access control
  • Usage history and audit log management
  • On-premise and private cloud deployment review
  • Integration with existing document stores and business systems

Data & Industry

Data and industries

  • Internal policies and operational manuals
  • Technical documents and research materials
  • Contracts, reports, and meeting notes
  • Customer support records and internal wikis
  • Finance, manufacturing, public sector, research institutions, and high-security enterprises

Outcome

What you gain

Less document search time

Quickly find scattered documents and knowledge, cutting staff's repetitive search time.

Permission-based knowledge use

Control which documents and answers are accessible based on user roles.

Reduced security burden

Minimize external transfer of sensitive data and build an AI usage structure suited to your environment.

Operational manageability

Usage history and logs let security officers and managers monitor how it's used.

How we work

We confirm security requirements first

We lock down the data boundary and access model early, then expand scope gradually.

  1. Security requirement review

    We confirm data tiers, network isolation, access rules, and audit log needs.

  2. Define knowledge scope

    We organize which documents, policies, and stores to connect first, and per-user exposure.

  3. Closed-environment validation

    We validate search accuracy, answer quality, and access policy on real documents.

  4. Build & operate

    We deploy an operable system with user interfaces, admin features, permissions, and logs.
